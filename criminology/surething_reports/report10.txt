KEY FINDING -- MOMENTUM IS EVERYTHING AT THE JURISDICTION LEVEL:
clearance_rate_ma3 (3-yr moving average) accounts for 63% of total SHAP signal.
This means: a state's near-term clearance future is overwhelmingly determined by
where it has been recently. Structural change (reporting quality, staffing,
detective workload) matters, but momentum dominates.

Implication for intervention design: interventions that reset momentum (sustained
process change over 3+ years) will outperform one-time shock interventions.
Philadelphia's 4-year trajectory (+40 pts) is consistent with this finding.

Compare to Phase 2B (case-level): solve_rate_lag1 was #1 SHAP at the case level.
At the jurisdiction level, the 3-yr MA supersedes it -- individual case noise
washes out; the systemic trend signal clarifies.

INTERVENTION FINDINGS:
MOMENTUM SUPPRESSION PARADOX -- CRITICAL FINDING:
Most states show NEGATIVE Y1 response to all interventions before recovering.
This is the clearance_rate_ma3 dominating: the model's Y1 prediction is anchored
to the 3-yr MA of prior trajectory. If a state is on a declining trend (Illinois,
Maryland, Indiana, Florida), interventions cannot immediately reverse the momentum
signal -- the MA must "catch up" to the new reality over 2-3 years.
Illinois: all three interventions show Y1 BELOW baseline (58.x vs 61.8%).
This is model behavior reflecting real dynamics -- institutional change takes time
to register in trend statistics.
POSITIVE ROI STATES (Add5yr > 0):
Georgia: +127-130 additional closures over 5 years (best ROI in priority set)
Michigan: +37-60 additional closures (consistent across all interventions)
Texas: +74 closures from reporting improvement specifically
These three states have positive momentum (above-trend recent performance) -- the
model correctly projects that interventions amplify existing positive trajectories.
ZERO-ROI STATES are declining-momentum states (Illinois, Maryland, Indiana):
The model is not saying interventions don't work in these states -- it's saying
these interventions as modeled aren't large enough to overcome negative momentum
within 5 years. The clearance_rate_ma3 anchor needs 3+ years of sustained
improvement before Add5yr turns positive. Philadelphia (4-year trajectory) is
the empirical proof that sustained intervention can break negative momentum.
INTERVENTION RANKING by type:
Best: Reporting completeness (+10 pts) -- positive ROI in Michigan, Georgia, Texas
Moderate: Solve_rate shock (+5 pts) -- best for Georgia
Weakest: Volume reduction (-20%) -- strongest mechanical effect but momentum offset
NOTE: gang_gun_share and urban_share are zeroed out in this run (missing from parquet).
Rerunning with those features may shift rankings materially.
